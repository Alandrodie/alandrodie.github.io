Méthode 1 : Optical flow and superpixel

In this page we will explain my algorithm that combines the result of a SLIC Superpixel algorithm and a Optical Flow

The first thing to do is to compute the Superpixels. The superpixels consist in segmenting the image into regions of equivalent size. We use the SLIC method. Basically, SLIC compute centers of the superpixels, affect close pixels to this superpixel and update the centers. This operations is repeated until there is a Convergence.

The Superpixels are use to determine the boundaries of the objects

The second operation to do is a optical flow between two frames. Because we need all the pixels to be associated with an optical flow we compute a Dense Optical Flow using the Farneback algorithm.

The optical flows are use to determine the position of moving objecs

when there is camera movements, the optical flow could be biaised and detect background as moving object. So this a a pre process after the optical flow concisting on removing the optical flows (magnitude and angle) close to the median flow in the image.

Finally, we have to use the boundaries provided by the Superpixels and the moving object provided by the optical flow . The algorithm is simple. For every superpixels, if there is a certain purcent (70%) of not null flow, the superpixel is set as object and added to our result.

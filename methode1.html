Method 1 : Optical flow and superpixel

In this page i will explain the algorithm that combines the result of the SLIC Superpixel algorithm and theOptical Flow

The first thing to do is to compute the Superpixels. The superpixels consist in segmenting the image into regions of equivalent size. We use the SLIC method. Basically, SLIC compute centers of the superpixels, affect close pixels to this superpixel and update the centers. This operations is repeated until there is a Convergence.

The Superpixels are used to determine the boundaries of the objects

The second operation is to do the optical flow between two frames. Because we need all the pixels to be associated with an optical flow, we compute a Dense Optical Flow using the Farneback algorithm.

The optical flows are use to determine the position of moving objecs

When there is camera movements, the optical flow could be biaised and detect background as moving object. So this we do a process after the optical flow which consist on removing the optical flows (magnitude and angle) close to the median flow in the image.

Finally, we have to use the boundaries provided by the Superpixels and the moving object provided by the optical flow . The algorithm is simple. For every superpixels, if there is a certain percent (70%) of not null flow, the superpixel is set as object and added to our result.
